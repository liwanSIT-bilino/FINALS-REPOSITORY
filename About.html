About Mountain Province

Mountain Province, nestled in the heart of the Cordillera Administrative Region in Northern Luzon, is a place where culture, nature, and history blend into a uniquely captivating identity. Known for its dramatic mountain ranges, mist-covered valleys, and centuries-old traditions, the province offers a serene escape from fast-paced city life and stands as one of the most culturally preserved areas in the Philippines. Its landscapes—ranging from towering pine-covered ridges to sprawling rice terraces carved by Indigenous hands—create a picture of beauty shaped by both nature and the resilience of its people. The province is composed of ten municipalities, including its capital, Bontoc, which serves as the cultural and administrative center. Each municipality has its own set of attractions and traditions, but all are deeply rooted in the heritage of the Igorot peoples, particularly the Bontoc, Kankanaey, and Applai tribes. These communities have preserved many of their ancient practices—from intricate weaving techniques and traditional farming systems to rituals honoring ancestors and nature spirits. This cultural richness is one of the defining traits that make Mountain Province a living museum of Cordilleran identity. Equally remarkable is the province’s natural beauty. The municipality of Sagada, for example, is internationally known for its majestic limestone caves, hanging coffins, and breathtaking sunrise views from Kiltepan. Meanwhile, towns like Bauko, Tadian, and Sadanga showcase expansive rice terraces that mirror the engineering genius of Indigenous communities who mastered the mountains long before modern tools existed. These landscapes not only support agriculture but also powerfully demonstrate how the people and the environment coexist in harmony. Mountain Province’s climate—cool, refreshing, and often blanketed in fog—adds to its charm. Its high elevations make it an ideal place for relaxation, reflection, and nature exploration. Visitors are drawn not only by its scenic attractions but also by its peaceful atmosphere and the warmth of its people, who remain grounded in kinship, community cooperation, and respect for tradition.
