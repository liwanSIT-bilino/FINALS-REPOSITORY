Welcome to Mountain Province

Welcome to Mountain Province, a land where towering mountains embrace peaceful valleys, where traditions live on through generations, and where every road leads to a story worth remembering. Nestled in the central highlands of the Cordillera region, Mountain Province is a destination where culture, nature, and community come together to create an experience unlike any other in the Philippines. Here, time moves a little slower. The cool mountain breeze carries the scent of pine trees, the sound of flowing rivers, and the echoes of ancient customs carefully preserved by the Indigenous peoples of the province. Whether you are wandering the historic streets of Bontoc, exploring the mystical caves of Sagada, or marveling at the hand-carved rice terraces in Sadanga and Bauko, you are stepping into a place shaped by harmony between people and nature. A Home for Culture and Heritage Mountain Province is home to the proud Igorot communities—known for their resilience, craftsmanship, and deep connection to the land. Their traditions, dances, rituals, and stories are woven into everyday life. As you explore the province, you’ll discover weaving centers, local farms, sacred sites, and beautifully preserved practices that reflect a culture flourishing in the modern world while honoring its roots. A Landscape That Inspires Adventure From fog-draped mountains to crystal-clear rivers, Mountain Province offers endless opportunities for adventure. Go trekking through limestone formations, witness breathtaking sunrises from iconic viewpoints, take peaceful walks through pine forests, or dive into the refreshing waters of natural falls. Each municipality offers its own unique wonders waiting to be explored. A Place That Feels Like Home More than its scenery, Mountain Province is beloved for its people. Visitors often speak of the warmth, humility, and hospitality of the locals—qualities that make every traveler feel welcomed. Whether you’re sharing a cup of brewed mountain coffee, learning about local customs, or simply enjoying the quiet charm of the highlands, you’ll feel a sense of belonging that keeps many visitors returning.
